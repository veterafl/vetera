# Romania Business Strategy

A 1–3 person, capital-light, niche business targeting the Romanian market via the diaspora wedge.

---

## TL;DR — The Recommendation

**Build "Lemne pentru Părinți"** — a service that diaspora Romanians pay to ensure their elderly parents in one specific Romanian county have firewood for winter. Sourced, delivered, measured, stacked, photographed for the diaspora customer. No app — website + WhatsApp + Stripe is the full stack.

- **Customer:** Diaspora son/daughter (in US/UK/IT/DE/ES) with elderly parent(s) in rural Romania
- **End-user:** The elderly parent in Romania
- **Pricing:** €500–600 per delivery, ~€180–280 margin per order
- **Realistic scale:** 200–1,000 customers in one county → €36k–250k annual revenue
- **Strategic purpose:** Wedge into a full **diaspora eldercare concierge** business in 18–24 months

---

## Strategic Framework

### Constraints
- **Team size:** 1–3 people total
- **No local Romanian business connections** (founder is Romanian-American, in US, no embedded network in RO)
- **No mass-market scale** — reject ideas requiring big ops, large teams, or two-sided marketplace liquidity
- **No AI-as-the-product** — that space is saturated
- **Monopoly = dominate a tiny niche** (Thiel)
- **Structural advantage over absolute effort** (Henderson)
- **Ride a 10-year wave** (Jensen)

### The Competitive Asymmetry
The founder is **Romanian-in-the-US**. This is a real advantage only when the business sits *on the diaspora side*: sells in EUR/USD to people who think like the founder, operates in RON in Romania, bridges the two worlds. Any business that doesn't leverage this asymmetry throws away the only structural edge.

## Romania: Market Context

### What people complain about
- **Bureaucracy:** ANAF, notar, primărie, court delays, SUMAL
- **Healthcare:** broken public hospitals, fragmented private clinics, doctor emigration
- **Services:** no quality signal for trades, doctors, lawyers, contractors
- **Trust:** OLX scams, landlord abuse, used-car fraud, delivery shortages
- **Housing:** Cluj/Bucharest unaffordability, new-build quality issues
- **Politics:** corruption, Georgescu aftermath, election volatility

### What people desire
- **"Romanian, but premium"** — quality Romanian-made goods that aren't kitschy
- **Țară / rural retreat** — restored traditional houses, slow living
- **Leave / come back with status** — emigration coaching or diaspora return concierge
- **Look young / look fit** — aesthetic medicine, dental tourism
- **Their kids ahead** — private education, international school, English from age 3

### What Romanians are obsessed with
1. Real estate (96% home ownership, highest in EU)
2. German cars (Audi/BMW/Mercedes as status)
3. Kids' academic success + emigration prospects
4. Parents, family, "ai mei"
5. Țară / rural roots
6. Food and hosting
7. Saving in gold + cash (deep bank distrust)
8. Diaspora comparison (constant)
9. Health, pharmacies, supplements
10. Hair transplants + dental + aesthetic procedures

### The #1 structural issue: Demographic Collapse
- Romania lost ~4M people since 1990 (23M → 19M, still falling)
- Birth rate ~1.4, lowest-ever year cohort
- Best, youngest, most-educated emigrate
- 28k Romanian doctors abroad — more than work in RO public system
- Cascades into healthcare collapse, education decline, pension crisis, labor shortage, rural depopulation
- **Implication:** never bet on "more Romanians." Bet on (a) more diaspora with more money, (b) more aging Romanians needing care with kids abroad, (c) more wealthy returners / Western expats discovering RO.

## Business Ideas Evaluated and Rejected

### ❌ Food delivery / restaurant / grocery app
- Saturated (Glovo, Tazz, Bolt Food)
- Two-sided marketplace = $50M+ capital business
- Negative margins for years
- No structural advantage for small team

### ❌ Car rental app for international travelers
- US tourists to RO: only ~80–150k/year, mostly one-time visitors
- TAM ~€10–50M, owned by Booking/Rentalcars/Sixt
- Capital intensive (fleet) or impossible distribution (aggregator)
- Zero customer LTV (one-time)

### ❌ American English tutoring app for Romanians
- Cambly, Preply, iTalki, Lingoda already dominate
- AI eating the category from below (Duolingo Max, ChatGPT)
- No diaspora wedge — anyone can build it
- Reverse direction (Romanian for diaspora kids) is much better

### ❌ State-bureaucracy intermediation (originally proposed as "#1 need")
- Too broad — "all Romanians hate ANAF" is too horizontal
- Service business with no experience curve (Henderson)
- Often requires licensed contabil/avocat to actually execute
- Trust takes years with no distribution

### ❌ "Wood-buying app"
- Wrong form factor: customer base (rural, 50+) doesn't use apps
- Supplier base is half gray-economy, resists platform legibility
- Hyperlocal logistics, no scale economics
- Right shape = **physical service, not app** (see recommendation)

## Ideas Worth Considering (Ranked Alternatives)

If "Lemne pentru Părinți" is rejected, these are the next-best fits — all share the diaspora-wedge + trust + niche pattern:

| Idea | Strengths | Weaknesses |

| **Aging-parents eldercare concierge (full version)** | Highest LTV, deepest moat | Heavier emotional + operational load than wood wedge |

## The Recommended Business: Lemne pentru Părinți

### The Offer (Verbatim)

> "Pay €500 by August 15. We deliver 5 ster of dry hardwood to your mother's address in [county] by October 1. We measure it in front of her. We stack it where she wants it. We send you photos and video. If anything goes wrong, we fix it or refund. We handle the supplier, the paperwork, the cash, all of it."

### Why This Wedge Wins

| Wedge property | Why it matters |
|---|---|
| **Time-bound urgency** (deadline: before winter) | Forces a decision, prevents endless deliberation |
| **Tangible outcome** (photo of stacked pile) | Trust built in one transaction |
| **One-shot purchase** | Low commitment to try the service |
| **Annually recurring** | Guaranteed re-engagement next year |
| **Emotionally loaded** ("mom freezing") | Low price sensitivity |
| **Naturally upsellable** | Bridges to chimney sweep → winter check-ins → year-round eldercare |
| **You ARE the customer profile** | Founder is diaspora, understands the guilt firsthand |

### The Customer Pain (Why diaspora kids pay)

The elderly parent will keep buying from "the local guy." The diaspora child is the customer because they fear:
- Mom getting shorted on quantity (5 ster ordered → 3.5 delivered is industry-standard scam)
- Wet wood → bad heat, chimney fires
- Pile left unstacked → rots, freezes, unusable
- SUMAL paperwork legal risk on elderly
- Suppliers no-showing in October when prices spike 2x
- Mom alone with strangers in yard
- Life-or-death stakes: hypothermia, CO poisoning

## Market Sizing

### Funnel
| Layer | Number |
|---|---|
| Romanian population | ~19M |
| Rural population | ~9.7M |
| Households heating with wood | ~1.5M HHs (~50% of all HHs) |
| Rural HHs heating with wood | 80–82% of rural HHs |
| Rural elderly HHs heating with wood (raw market) | ~400–600k HHs |
| With at least one child in diaspora | ~160–300k HHs |
| Diaspora child can/will pay €500/yr | **~50,000–150,000 HHs (national TAM)** |
| Per single county (e.g., Maramureș or Suceava) | **5,000–10,000 addressable families** |

### Realistic Capture
- **Year 1 in one county:** 0.5–2% = 25–200 customers
- **Year 3 with reputation:** 5–15% = 250–1,500 customers
- **Steady state per county:** 1,000+ customers achievable

### Why this market size is a feature, not a bug
- Too small to attract VC competition
- Big enough to support a real 1–3 person business
- Henderson sweet spot — incumbents won't fight you for it

## Operating Model

### Geographic focus
Pick **one county** where wood-heating + heavy-diaspora overlap is strongest:
- **Maramureș** (Italy/Spain/UK diaspora, mountain wood-heating villages)
- **Suceava / Bucovina** (massive Italy diaspora)
- **Bistrița-Năsăud** (Germany diaspora)
- **Harghita / Covasna** (Hungarian-speaking, concentrated)
- **Neamț, Bacău, Vâlcea, Argeș, Hunedoara** (secondary options)

Avoid: south/east lowlands (less wood heating, more gas/electricity).

### Team
- **You (in US):** sales, marketing, customer comms, payments, coordination — ~10–15 hrs/week peak season
- **One supervisor (in RO):** local trusted person who verifies deliveries, takes photos, manages stacker. €800–1,200/mo + per-delivery bonus.
- **Stackers (day labor):** €40/job, on-call network
- **Wood suppliers (existing businesses):** 2–3 vetted partners, SUMAL-compliant

### What you own
- Brand + website + customer list
- Supplier relationships
- Reputation in diaspora communities

### What you DON'T own
- Trucks, warehouses, wood inventory
- Real estate
- An app
- A team larger than 3 people

### Tech Stack (under €500/year total)
| Tool | Purpose |
|---|---|
| One-page website (Carrd / Framer / Webflow) | Sell service, take orders |
| Stripe Checkout | Payments |
| WhatsApp Business | Customer comms |
| Google Sheets | Order tracking |
| Google Drive | Photo/video proof storage |
| Mailchimp / Buttondown | Annual customer email |

**No app. Ever. Unless year 5+ and 5,000+ customers.**

## Launch Plan

### Phase 1: Setup (May–June 2026)
- Pick county (use any personal thread, even a cousin)
- Register micro-SRL in RO (or work as US LLC initially)
- Build one-page website (Romanian + Italian + English + Spanish translations)
- Negotiate with 2–3 wood suppliers
- Hire supervisor (this is the hardest hire — reference, reference, reference)
- Set up Stripe, business email, WhatsApp Business

### Phase 2: Sales Season (July–August 2026)
- Join 5–10 diaspora Facebook groups for chosen county
- Post not as ads — as personal stories ("Anul trecut părinții mei...")
- Goal: 20–50 first-season orders
- Take payments upfront via Stripe

### Phase 3: Delivery Season (September–October 2026)
- 80% of annual work compressed here
- Supervisor runs 5–15 deliveries/week
- Photo/video proof sent to every customer in real time
- Collect testimonials immediately after each delivery

### Phase 4: Retention (November–December 2026)
- Send paper holiday card to every parent (real card, mailed)
- Thank-you message to every diaspora customer
- Ask: "anything else we can help with this winter?"

### Phase 5: Expansion (Q1 2027)
- Upsell: chimney sweep, snow shoveling, weekly winter check-ins
- Begin transition to year-round eldercare concierge
- Build referral program for next wood season

## Path to Evolution: From Wood to Full Eldercare

The wood business is **v1**. The compounding business is full eldercare concierge for diaspora families.

| Stage | Service | Revenue per customer |
|---|---|---|
| **v1** (2026) | Annual firewood | €500/year |
| **v2** (2026–27) | + chimney sweep + winter check-ins | +€200–400/year |
| **v3** (2027) | + spring garden + summer paperwork + medical accompaniment | +€1,500/year |
| **v4** (2027–28) | Full eldercare subscription (weekly visits, comprehensive care) | €3,000–6,000/year |

By 2028: 300 active families × €3,000 avg = **€900k annual revenue**, 2–3 person team, owned 100%, defensible by reputation.

The wood transaction is the *cheapest possible first proof of trust*. Everything after is expansion to the same customer base.

## Key Risks

| Risk | Mitigation |
|---|---|
| Supervisor quits / underperforms | Pay well, document everything, build relationship before scaling |
| Wood supplier shorts you / sells illegal wood | Vet 2–3 suppliers, verify SUMAL, build measurement protocol |
| Bad delivery → diaspora customer loses trust | Refund fast, fix immediately, document for case studies |
| Cold start: no first customers | Free pilots for 3 families in your personal network; collect testimonials before charging |
| One parent has serious health incident on your watch | Insurance from day one; clear scope (this is wood + check-in, not medical care) |
| Regulatory change (SUMAL crackdown, price caps) | Operate fully legally from day one; political risk is real |
| Seasonal cash gap (Q1–Q2) | Use upsell services to smooth, or treat as part-time founder business |
| Emotional weight of working with elderly | Build supervisor's capacity for this; have boundaries |

## What "Building This" Actually Looks Like

This is **not a tech business**. There's no:
- Pitch deck
- VC funding
- App Store launch
- Demo day
- Engineering sprint

There IS:
- A founder writing emails to suppliers in Romanian
- A founder posting in Facebook groups at midnight US time
- A supervisor driving to a village to measure a wood pile in October rain
- A WhatsApp video of stacked wood being sent to a daughter in Milan
- A €500 Stripe payment landing in your bank account in July

This is a **small, physical, trust-based business**. Boring on the outside. Compounding on the inside. Owned 100%. Defensible by reputation alone. Exactly the shape a 1–3 person team can dominate.
